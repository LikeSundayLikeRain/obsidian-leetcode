<objective>
Close `G-DROPDOWN-CLIPPED` (queued in `05.3-UAT.md` `## Open follow-ups`): the chevron dropdown opens visually but its items are unhittable because the dropdown is rendered inside CM6's `cm-content` element, which has `contain: paint`. `contain: paint` clips descendants AND breaks hit-testing for any descendant that overflows the cm-content box, regardless of `z-index` or `isolation`. Plan 07's `z-index: 9999` + `isolation: isolate` did not fix this because `contain: paint` overrides stacking-order hit-testing rules.

Purpose: Restore the load-bearing language-switching UX by portaling the dropdown into `document.body` with absolute coordinates derived from the chevron button's `getBoundingClientRect()`, so the dropdown lives outside the cm-content paint container and is hit-testable in its own right.

Output: Refactored `src/main/languageChevronWidget.ts` whose `openDropdown` attaches the dropdown to `document.body` and whose `closeDropdown` detaches it; updated `tests/main/languageChevronWidget.test.ts` whose assertions reflect the portal pattern; live-smoke checkpoint task confirms hit-test resolution in the dev vault.

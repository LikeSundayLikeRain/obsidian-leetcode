## Arity Threading Decision

**Used:** `cases.length` (positive-int passthrough).

**Why not metaData-aware:** RunModal's `RunModalArgs` interface (lines 37-42) carries `slug`, `exampleTestcases`, `store`, `onRun` — no `metaData` and no `sampleTestCase`. Wiring those through would touch `main.ts` (the modal's call sites) and is explicitly out of scope per Plan 02's `<action>` ("if neither metaData nor sampleTestCase is plumbed into RunModal today, fallback to `arity = this.cases.length`").

**Why it's still correct:** `joinCasesForRun(cases, _arity)` in `src/solve/runArity.ts` (lines 138-148) marks the arity parameter as `_arity` and does not consume it — the JOIN direction is just `cases.map(trim).filter(non-empty).join('\n')`. Arity matters for the SPLIT direction (Plan 03's per-case parse of `code_answer[]`), not for the JOIN. Passing `this.cases.length` is a positive integer which satisfies the `Number.isFinite(arity) && arity > 0` defensiveness in any future arity-aware shaping.
